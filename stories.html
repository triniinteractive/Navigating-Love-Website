Kaori Miyazono and Kosei Arima

Kaori Miyazono and Kōsei Arima’s love story is one of inspiration, courage, and bittersweet truth. Kōsei, once a piano prodigy, had lost his way after the death of his mother, unable to hear the sound of his own playing and trapped in silence and grief. Into this quiet world burst Kaori, a free‑spirited violinist whose music was wild, emotional, and untamed. She lived with a vibrancy that challenged every rule Kōsei had clung to, and her presence became the spark that reignited his passion for life. Kaori pushed him back onto the stage, not with gentle encouragement but with fearless insistence, reminding him that music — like love — is meant to be felt, not perfected. Their bond grew through performances, laughter, and shared struggles, even as Kaori secretly battled a devastating illness. Though her time was short, Kaori’s influence was eternal: she taught Kōsei to embrace vulnerability, to fight for joy, and to understand that love is not measured in years but in the depth of connection. Her final letter, confessing that she had loved him all along, became her “lie in April,” a truth wrapped in tenderness that left Kōsei forever changed.
